Effects caused

When this glitch Pokémon is forced on the opponent's side, it is capable of causing a glitch battle. This glitch battle is surprisingly more stable than some of the others, allowing the player to scroll through a glitched inventory and be sent back to the overworld in a Glitch City after choosing Fight or Run. Curiously, it may not be possible to use any of the items and it is possible to glitch the cursor position on the inventory as well.

The game may freeze after the player is sent to the Glitch City. Furthermore it is possible that the party Pokémon data is glitched, because opening the party Pokémon menu after the glitch may cause the game to freeze with a white screen (typical of an unterminated party list or zero maximum HP glitch).

When this glitch Pokémon is viewed in the Pewter Museum of Science by forcing RAM address CF95 to 0xE6 (e.g. with GameShark code 01E695CF), it is capable of heavily corrupting the overworld and forcing a glitch text box as well.
